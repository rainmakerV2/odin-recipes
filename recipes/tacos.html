Lasagna Recipe

Description

Tacos are the ultimate crowd-pleaser, combining seasoned ground beef, fresh toppings, and warm tortillas into a handheld feast that's perfect for any night of the week. This classic beef taco recipe is quick, easy, and endlessly customizable, making it ideal for family dinners or casual gatherings with friends. The beauty of tacos lies in their versatility—everyone can build their own perfect combination.

The secret to exceptional tacos starts with well-seasoned beef cooked until perfectly browned and flavorful. Combined with a colorful array of fresh toppings and your choice of soft or crunchy shells, these tacos deliver satisfying texture and bold flavors in every bite. Best of all, you can have dinner on the table in under 30 minutes, making this recipe a go-to option for busy weeknights when you want something delicious without spending hours in the kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1/2 teaspoon oregano
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper (optional)
  • 1/2 cup water or beef broth
  • 8-10 taco shells or flour tortillas
  • Shredded lettuce
  • Diced tomatoes
  • Shredded cheddar cheese
  • Sour cream
  • Salsa
  • Sliced jalapeños (optional)
  • Chopped cilantro
  • Lime wedges

Steps

  • Heat olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the diced onion and cook for 3-4 minutes until softened and translucent.
  • Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Add the ground beef to the skillet, breaking it up with a wooden spoon. Cook for 5-7 minutes until the meat is browned and no longer pink. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  • Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, oregano, salt, black pepper, and cayenne pepper (if using). Mix well to coat the meat evenly with the spices.
  • Pour in the water or beef broth and stir to combine. Reduce heat to medium-low and simmer for 5-7 minutes until the liquid has mostly evaporated and the mixture has thickened.
  • While the meat simmers, warm the taco shells in the oven according to package directions, or heat flour tortillas in a dry skillet for about 30 seconds per side.
  • Prepare all your toppings by shredding the lettuce, dicing the tomatoes, grating the cheese, and arranging everything in serving bowls.
  • Taste the taco meat and adjust seasoning with additional salt or spices if needed.
  • Spoon the seasoned beef into the warmed taco shells or tortillas.
  • Set out all the toppings and let everyone build their tacos with their favorite combinations. Serve with lime wedges on the side for squeezing over the top.
