Lasagna Recipe



Description

Lasagna is the ultimate Italian comfort food, featuring layers of tender pasta, rich meat sauce, creamy béchamel, and melted cheese that come together in perfect harmony. This traditional recipe has been beloved for generations, offering a satisfying meal that's perfect for family dinners, special occasions, or meal prep for the week ahead. The combination of savory ground beef, aromatic herbs, and tangy tomato sauce creates a depth of flavor that improves even more the next day.

What makes this lasagna truly special is the balance of textures and flavors in every bite. The pasta provides structure, the meat sauce brings heartiness and warmth, while the creamy layers add richness that ties everything together. Whether you're making this for a crowd or enjoying leftovers throughout the week, this classic lasagna delivers restaurant-quality results right from your own kitchen. It does require some time and effort, but the impressive final dish is well worth it.

Ingredients

  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1/2 pound Italian sausage
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 (28-ounce) can crushed tomatoes
  • 2 (6-ounce) cans tomato paste
  • 1 (15-ounce) can tomato sauce
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 2 tablespoons sugar
  • 2 teaspoons dried basil
  • 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  • 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 (16-ounce) container ricotta cheese
  • 1 egg
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
  • 12 lasagna noodles
  • 4 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese

Steps

  • In a large pot over medium heat, cook the ground beef, Italian sausage, onion, and garlic until the meat is browned and the onion is translucent, about 10 minutes. Drain excess fat.
  • Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, tomato sauce, and water to the pot. Stir in the sugar, basil, fennel seeds, Italian seasoning, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • While the sauce simmers, cook the lasagna noodles according to package directions. Drain and rinse with cold water to prevent sticking. Set aside.
  • In a medium bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, egg, and parsley. Mix until well blended and set aside.
  • Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Spread 1 1/2 cups of meat sauce in the bottom of a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  • Arrange 4 lasagna noodles over the sauce, overlapping slightly if needed. Spread half of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then sprinkle with 1 1/3 cups mozzarella and 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese. Top with 1 1/2 cups of meat sauce.
  • Repeat the layering process: 4 noodles, remaining ricotta mixture, 1 1/3 cups mozzarella, 1/4 cup Parmesan, and 1 1/2 cups meat sauce.
  • Add the final layer of 4 noodles and top with the remaining meat sauce. Sprinkle with the remaining mozzarella and Parmesan cheese.
  • Cover the baking dish tightly with aluminum foil (spray the foil with cooking spray to prevent cheese from sticking) and bake for 25 minutes.
  • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown. Let the lasagna rest for 15 minutes before slicing and serving.
